Join Us!

RUMT would love to have any Rice undergraduate student who is interested in Mock Trial. Even if you think you have no prior experience, we would love for you to come to a meeting and learn more about the team and what exactly it means to be a part of RUMT. Many people think that Mock Trial is only for people who want to become lawyers in the future, but this really isnâ€™t the case. If you have an interest in or experience with theatre or debate, Mock Trial may be a good fit for you.

The team will begin meeting in the fall of each school year. Information about interest meetings will be posted on our Twitter (@MockRice, link in the "About" section). For additional information, feel free to contact us through Twitter. RUMT will be competing in invitationals and scrimmages in the fall, so it is a great time for new members to try out their skills in a more casual environment. If you wish to be a part of the team that will participate in AMTA tournaments in the spring (Regionals, ORCs, and Nationals), then you should come to the fall meetings to learn about the case and figure out which role is the best fit for you.

About

Rice University Mock Trial is Rice University's fully student-run mock trial program. Rice Mock Trial was founded in Fall of 2017. In Spring of 2018, Rice Mock Trial won its first team-wide award when it traveled to Sherman, Texas for Kangaroo Brawl and placed third in the competition. Soon after, the team attended the UT Dallas Regional Tournament and won enough ballots to advance to the next round of competition, the Opening Round Championships Series.

In the fall of 2018, RUMT took on 27 new students (a six-fold increase in membership) and hosted its first tournament, RiceBowl 2018! By the end of fall semester invitationals, five members of the team had received outstanding witness awards. In spring of 2019, RUMT sent teams to California and Oklahoma where Rice placed third at the Sooner invitational.

Despite being one of the youngest teams in the country, Rice Mock Trial has earned bids to compete at the Opening Round Championships Series every year of its existence. One of Rice Mock Trial's competitive goals for the 2019-2020 tournament cycle is for the team to make its debut appearance in the National Championship while continuing to develop the skills of its newest members.
